Detailed Insights and Recommendations

PROBLEM STATEMENT 1: Identify and Understand High-Value Customers

Which Country has the most Invoices?

  • USA leads with 91 invoices, followed by Canada (58), France (35), and Brazil (35).
  • Roughly 15 countries have equal invoice counts (7).
Insight: The USA and Canada represent the primary customer markets.
Recommendation: Prioritise events, ads, and product launches in the USA and Canada; use regional content strategies in lower-volume countries to increase awareness and engagement.

Which cities have the best customers?

This supports the Event Manager in planning promotional music festivals in the cities with the best customers.

  • Prague has the highest-value customer (£90), clearly outperforming others.
  • Mountain View, Paris, São Paulo, and London show similar performance (~£75).
Insight: Prague is an outlier market with a high-spending individual.
Recommendation: Target Prague for VIP events, exclusive merchandise, or ambassador partnerships; treat other cities as strong secondary markets for touring and promotions.

Who are the best customers?

Customers who has spent the most money in the history of the music store.

  • Frank Harris and Mark Philips are top spenders, followed by Helena Hola and Richard Cunningham.
  • Several customers contribute roughly equally in the mid-tier range (~£45–£50).
Insight: A mix of high-value and consistent mid-tier customers.
Recommendation: Offer exclusive perks to top-tier customers (early access, VIP seating); introduce tiered loyalty programmes to retain mid-tier customers and increase spending.

Invoices count per country (top-spending customer)

Each country represents the single customer who has spent the most in that country.

  • Shows top-spending customers across multiple countries with values fairly close.
Insight: Strong individual engagement is spread evenly across countries.
Recommendation: Recognise these users with “Top Fan in Your Country” campaigns; develop localised influencer partnerships or spotlight content.

PROBLEM STATEMENT 2: Understand Music & Artist Preference and Behaviour

Top artists by invoice total count

Find which artist has the most according to the InvoiceLine count.

  • Iron Maiden (140) leads, followed by U2 (107), Metallica (91), and Led Zeppelin (87).
Insight: A handful of legendary rock artists dominate sales volume.
Recommendation: Focus festival headliners and playlist themes around these artists; bundle their tracks in box sets or “best of” collections.

Customers contributing to the top artist (Iron Maiden)

With Iron Maiden as the top artist by InvoiceLine, identify customers who spent the most on them.

  • Mark Taylor, Madalena Sampaio, and Hannah Schneider top the list.
  • Long-tail of contributors follows with < 5 purchases each.
Insight: A few highly dedicated fans drive artist-specific sales.
Recommendation: Use this for fan-club targeting and Iron Maiden-centric events/merch; encourage the long tail with exclusive offers.

Rock music listeners by country

  • USA (13) and Canada (8) have the highest number of unique Rock listeners.
Insight: Rock has a strong foothold in North America.
Recommendation: Focus Rock merchandise, marketing, and touring in the USA and Canada; run audience-development campaigns in lower-engagement countries.

Top artists with the most Rock tracks

Supports decisions on inviting rock artists to events or inventory planning.

  • Leaders: Led Zeppelin, U2, Deep Purple, Iron Maiden.
Insight: Clear descending popularity trend.
Recommendation: Prioritise these artists for festival headliners; create themed playlists, merch bundles, and fan experiences around them.

Popular genre in each country

  • Rock dominates most countries (especially USA, Canada, France).
  • Latin popular in Spain; Alternative & Punk shows niche appeal in a few regions.
Insight: Preferences vary by country, though Rock is most preferred overall.
Recommendation: Curate region-specific setlists for festivals; run genre-based marketing aligned with local tastes (e.g., rock-heavy in USA, Latin-heavy in Spain).

PROBLEM STATEMENT 3: Optimise Product Offering Based on Media Type & Album Popularity

Most popular media type

  • MPEG Audio File dominates the catalogue.
  • Other types (AAC, Protected AAC, MPEG-4) trail far behind.
Insight: Most users consume standard audio formats.
Recommendation: Focus inventory and digital licensing on MPEG audio; avoid overstocking less-used formats unless tied to exclusives (e.g., video or DRM-protected tracks).

Top 10 countries by total spend (media type & spending categories)

  • USA leads (≈ $3,830), followed by Canada, France, and Brazil.
  • Countries grouped into High and Medium Spenders.
Insight: Highest-revenue markets are clear; USA is prime for upsells and exclusives.
Recommendation: Focus high-budget campaigns in USA/Canada; target medium spenders with bundles or discounts to increase CLV.

Top 3 total sales & customer contribution for each year

Top 3 most-sold albums by year and customer counts (Max Date = 2013-12-22, Min Date = 2009-01-01).

  • Greatest Hits and The Office series are consistent top sellers across years.
  • Customer counts vary—revealing loyalty vs. broad-appeal trade-offs.
Insight: Some albums combine high revenue with broad reach; others rely on fewer but high-value buyers.
Recommendation: Promote Greatest Hits and best-sellers as premium packages; consider re-releases or bundles for new audiences.

Distribution of music greater than average track length

  • Most tracks (≈270) fall between 6.6 and 14.1 minutes; very few exceed 59 minutes.
Insight: Very long tracks are rare—useful for playlist and event programming.
Recommendation: Use short-to-medium tracks for general playlists; reserve longer tracks for exclusive/immersive experiences (e.g., live sessions, deluxe editions).
